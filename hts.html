Hubble space telescope


Launched on April 24, 1990, aboard the Space Shuttle Discovery, the Hubble Space Telescope revolutionized astronomy by providing clear, detailed images of distant galaxies, nebulae, and other celestial objects without the interference of Earth's atmosphere. Its observations have led to major discoveries, including the expansion rate of the universe, exoplanets, and insights into black holes. Hubble remains one of the most important tools in modern astrophysics.

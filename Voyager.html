Voyager 1 and 2


Voyager 1: Launched on September 5, 1977, Voyager 1 became the first spacecraft to reach interstellar space in 2012. It provided groundbreaking data on the outer planets, especially Jupiter and Saturn, and sent back iconic images, including the "Pale Blue Dot" photograph of Earth. Voyager 1 continues to transmit data about the interstellar environment, far beyond the solar system.
Voyager 2: Launched on August 20, 1977, Voyager 2 is the only spacecraft to have visited all four giant planets: Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. It provided invaluable insights into their moons, rings, and atmospheres.
